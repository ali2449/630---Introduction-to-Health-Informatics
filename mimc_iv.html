MIMIC-IV: The Premier Critical Care Database

What is MIMIC-IV?

MIMIC-IV (Medical Information Mart for Intensive Care IV) is a large, single-center database containing deidentified health-related data associated with patients admitted to the Beth Israel Deaconess Medical Center in Boston, Massachusetts. It represents the evolution of the MIMIC database, with significant improvements in data quality, organization, and documentation.

Comprehensive Coverage

Data from over 40,000 patients admitted to intensive care units between 2008-2019

Multi-modal Data

Structured EHR data, clinical notes, waveforms, and imaging data

Research Ready

Deidentified, curated, and documented for research use

International Standard

Used by thousands of researchers worldwide for clinical studies

Access Requirement: All MIMIC databases require completion of CITI training and PhysioNet credentialing. The core MIMIC-IV database serves as the foundation for all specialized subsets.

Specialized MIMIC-IV Subsets & Derivatives

The MIMIC-IV ecosystem has expanded to include numerous specialized subsets tailored for specific research domains. These curated datasets provide enriched annotations, processed data, and specialized labels that accelerate research in targeted areas.

Clinical Documentation & NLP Subsets

MIMIC-IV-Note

NLP Clinical

Deidentified clinical notes including discharge summaries, radiology reports, and physician notes with structured metadata.

  • Discharge summaries
  • Radiology reports
  • Physician notes
  • Nursing notes

Best for: Clinical NLP, information extraction, document classification

MIMIC-IV-ED

Descriptive Predictive

Emergency department data with comprehensive information on patient flow, triage, and acute care management.

  • Triage information
  • Chief complaints
  • ED medications
  • Disposition decisions

Best for: Emergency medicine research, throughput optimization

Diagnostic & Imaging Subsets

MIMIC-CXR

Diagnostic Imaging

Chest X-ray dataset with 377,110 images from 65,379 patients, paired with structured EHR data.

  • DICOM images
  • Radiology reports
  • Structured labels
  • Bounding boxes

Best for: Medical imaging AI, computer-aided diagnosis

MIMIC-IV-ECG

Diagnostic Predictive

Electrocardiogram data with waveform signals and clinical context for cardiovascular research.

  • ECG waveforms
  • Signal metadata
  • Clinical context
  • Quality indicators

Best for: Arrhythmia detection, signal processing

Process & Outcome Oriented Subsets

MIMIC-IV-ECG-Ext-ICD

Diagnostic Predictive

ECG dataset enhanced with ICD diagnostic labels for supervised learning and cardiovascular disease detection.

  • Diagnostic labels
  • Temporal alignment
  • Multi-label annotations
  • Clinical validation

Best for: Automated ECG interpretation, supervised learning

MIMIC-IV-Ext-CEKG

Descriptive Prescriptive

Process-oriented dataset with clinical event knowledge graphs for care pathway analysis.

  • Temporal event sequences
  • Clinical workflows
  • Outcome trajectories
  • Resource utilization

Best for: Process mining, care pathway optimization

Research Advantage: These specialized subsets save researchers significant time in data preprocessing and annotation, allowing focus on methodological innovation and clinical insights.
